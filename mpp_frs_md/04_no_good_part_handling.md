# Section 4 — No-Good Part (Reject) Handling Process

> **Source:** Madison Precision Products MES — Functional Requirement Specification (Flexware, v1.0, 3/15/2024)  

> **Status:** Reference document — Blue Ridge Automation does not have access to the SparkMES framework.


---


           4 N O -G OOD P ART (R E JEC T ) H A NDLIN G P RO CE SS
           Legacy MES does not manage or track No-Good (reject) Parts. The new MES solution will allow entry of
           reject counts in the context of the LOT they are from. MPP may elect to not record this information at all
           manufacturing steps.
           The New MES will include nonconformance tracking capabilities that will typically apply to parts that
           need further investigation and possible remediation. MES will allow operators to enter the count of No-
           Good (reject/scrap) parts associated with a specific LOT when appropriate and will allow the operator to
           enter a scrap reason and comment. This data will be retained for analysis but is not considered part of
           the permanent production records.
